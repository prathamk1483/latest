About Us

History

In Maharashtra, this idol of Siddhivinayak Ganapati is seen in two places. After Mumbai, this beautiful idol is found at Shri Shretra Waigaon in Amravati district. The history of this Siddhivinayak idol can be traced back to ancient stories where it is mentioned that the Pandavas, during their incognito stay in Virat's kingdom in the Mahabharata, came across this idol. The sight of this idol marked the end of their hidden exile in the forest. During the Mughal period, this idol was buried underground to protect it. The nearby villages of Achalpur, Darapur, and Kholapur were under Mughal control at the time. After several decades, the exact location of the idol was lost, and people longed to have a direct encounter with it. However, the idol remained undiscovered until it suddenly resurfaced in the Ingole family's house in Waigaon. This happened in the 16th century. From then on, a temple was built to honour the idol, and the Ingole family resumed their service to the idol. This tradition continues today, with millions of devotees visiting to Waigaon to see pray the Ganesh idol.

The Tale of the temple

Temple is managed by Ingole Family from initiation. Ancestor of Ingole Family Late Shri Sitaramji, Shri Shivramji, Shri Shriramji, Shri Tulshiramji donated some own land to temple on 25th July’23 with registration note to run the temple smoothly and celebration of Ganapati Festivals. Accordingly, temple managed from 1921 to 1930 by Late Shri Sitaramji, 1030 to 1940 – Shri Shivramji, 1940 to1960 – Shri Tulshiramji, Shri Tukaramji and 1960 to 1993 – Shri Dayaramji Ingole. Over time, due to the growing numbers of devotees, Late Shri Dayaramji Ingole suggested to formed temple trust for the convenience and to form facilities for devotees. Accordingly trust formation responsibility was given to Mr. Vilasrao Tulshiramji Ingole. After many difficulties, trust formed on 31st Aug’2020, till now from establishment of the trust Shri. Vilasrao Ingole served as the president of the trust and other nominated members of Ingole family as trustee. Subsequently, the construction of the new temple was undertaken from 2005 and completed in 2007. Every year, a grand celebration is held from Bhadrapada Shukla. 4 to Purnima, ten-day’s Ganesh chaturthi festival celebration takes place. It includes daily annadan, gyanadan, bhajans, kirtans, Kakda, and Haripatth. On Purnima, the immersion of the idol is performed with the beats of the mridang and the sounds of joy. Similarly Ganesh Jayanti festival celebrated every year in Magh shukla paksha for one week.

Uniqueness of the Idol.

This text describes a deity statue that is considered to be divinely beautiful and enchanting throughout the state of Maharashtra. The statue is made of white stone and is adorned with auspicious symbols, including a single tusk (Ekadanta), a lotus at the base of the feet, and conch shell markings. These symbols represent the six divine qualities (Shadaiwaryache Pratik). Devotees who witness this statue never feel lacking in anything. The statue has both hands adorned with flowers, signifying the attainment of Ashtamahasiddhi (eight superhuman powers), Anima, Garima, Laghima, and Mahima. The necklace and modak (sweet dumpling) in its hand symbolize material and spiritual prosperity. Those who have the privilege of seeing this incomparable beauty find themselves experiencing a deep sense of devotion and inner peace. It is believed that this deity fulfils the wishes of those who approach with devotion, bringing happiness and tranquillity to their lives. Even those who do not have children are blessed with offspring. The exquisite beauty of the statue cannot be compared to anything else, and it leaves a profound impact on rational and peaceful individuals. Therefore, during the transition between Uttarayana and Dakshinayana, the first rays of the rising sun fall on this statue. Some even consider this event as an auspicious entry into the year. In summary, the text describes the unique and divinely significant qualities of a deity statue, emphasizing the spiritual and material blessings it bestows on its devotees.
